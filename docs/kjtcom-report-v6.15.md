## Discovery Summary

Phase 6a successfully mapped the aesthetic and structural landscape of 8 key competitors. The discovery reinforces the "Dark/Terminal" aesthetic established in the Panther reference, but adds modern SaaS polish inspired by Scanner.dev.

### Key Findings

1. **Typography:** Scanner.dev's use of **Geist Sans** and **Geist Mono** is the superior choice for a modern, developer-centric data platform. kjtcom will adopt this stack.
2. **Pipeline Aesthetics:** Monad and Cribl provide the strongest structural parallels. The use of "nodes", "transforms", and "enrichment" in their UI matches the kjtcom backend logic.
3. **Data Density:** Maltego demonstrates high-density investigation UX. kjtcom should avoid over-simplification in the "Investigation" view.
4. **Editorial Polish:** Black Tomato proves that even "technical" data (travel itineraries) can be presented with high-end editorial authority.
